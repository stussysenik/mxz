It doesn't matter whether you're learning a language, a skill or you just enjoy the process of learning, whatever is the case I have a tip for you. Well, before I go into it, let's start with a motto that is very popular within the community of makers, doers, or all those so-called problem solvers. Don't get me wrong on this, but nowadays I feel like people are either CEOs or fall into one of these categories. At some point in your life, you must have heard something like this: "*Stop learning, and start creating.*"

Essentially, there is nothing wrong with this motto, but what many people don't realize is the fact that there is a hook to it, as new things are basically a remix of the past-known knowledge. Sooner or later, you will get stuck or run out of ideas to create, as our imaginations are limited. At one point, you might even become "tasteless" and run into the infinite loops of underappreciation or boredom. And let's face it, boredom is surely a killer of learning.

Therefore, I asked myself what would happen if we gave it a little twist:

> **Stop creating and start breaking**

Funnily enough, this can also be a learning path. You don't have to be an avid or passionate learner to break things apart since destruction is less effortful than creation, right? Personally, by taking things apart, I not only get inspired and learn how others do things but I also learn to understand. By learning from others, you will also learn the different perspectives of the same coin.

I personally think that learning from others is a foundational building block to my prosperity. Each of us is a unique individual, and by destructuring the work of others, you can find all those hidden gems that make us different and later tailor them based on your needs. **Please note that copying and elaborating on ideas aren't the two same things.** You shall not make exact replicas of the originator but only elaborate on it. In the world of dance, we refer to this concept as *flipping*. When you are flipping, you take an idea and make the idea yours by adding your distinct flavor to it, and the combination of flavors gives rise to something new.

#### So to take this into practice, I have listed out some practical examples:

* For developers, I suggest reading other people's code.
* If you are learning a language, try to dismantle an essay from a great writer. Start first with someone you highly appreciate, and then move to classical authors if possible.
* Are you an engineer? Try to break an old electronic device into parts.
* Math lovers, obviously, you won't able to "break" Math problems. What you can do is to do things the other way around. Already good at solving problems? Try to come up with some.

You may or may not like this approach, but to make things clear I'm not rediscovering learning, I'm just looking at it from a new perspective. If you happen to have a system that works, then I'm happy for you! 🤗
