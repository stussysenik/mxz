Lately, I'm into blogging for quite a lot. There is just something utterly pleasing when you can clearly write your thoughts and ideas out.

I'm not a life guru or your conventional self-help coach, don't ever consider me one, not at all. I'm just stating my feelings and ideas into words, nothing more nothing less. I'm not trying to reeducate you, if you feel like so, please close this tab immediately before it's too late. If you are still reading these lines, have a seat, and try to hear me out.

#### Think with your heart
From the very early on, I've figured out that no matter what you do, you do it with a heart and focus. Only until then, you allow yourself to fully grow. Also, I noticed that you should do things for yourself, not to make your parents happy, not to compare with others, you do things because you can. This is trivial, but it still needs your attention to remind yourself that. Sometimes, you use your heart to think, instead of your mind.

Even though not every single time you are in charge of things, well there are external factors that might affect you but you are always in charge of your reaction. You can always decide whether you will do something or not. Whether you will be angry or not. Whether you will apologize or not. Whether you will do the work or not. It even comes down to whether you will ask your crush out or not. Whatever is the case, it's only up to you to decide. But if you consecutively decide not to do so, then you shouldn't blame your potential failure on others. It truly is hard to acknowledge this in the middle of fierce frustration but remember, it was you who decided it. Take responsibility.

#### Hard work.
I want to be better, you want to be better, everybody wants to be better. Nothing change will ever change this, it's our collective urge. I want to change my future through my hard work. I know, oftentimes hard work won't necessarily let you reach the goal, but if you don't put enough work into it, you will never know. Even if I fail, I will be mentally happy cause I put enough work into it. To this point in my life, I believe that 90% percent of the things did work out for me (knock on wood). It shouldn't be your motivation to reach the goal through hard work, consider success as a side effect. No outcome is for certain or granted but your hard work is.

I think what many people are doing incorrectly is that they want to see results and good outcomes fast. It sounds very amiable but this mindset will ruin you in the long run. Personally, I'm in love with the process of working hard, as cliche as it sounds. I like the process of building blocks, of working on something with a bigger outtake. 
If you think about it, how many times you told yourself that you will be happy once you reach something? If I ever could do this, or that I would be for sure happy. If I had this, I will be happy. Yeah, with that approach you will be happy but not for so long.

I bet that all the enthusiasm and happiness was just momentary, in the end, you were left with a bland feeling of accomplishment. Work and do things (not necessarily hard), because you feel like so and you want, not to seek the goal. I know that I'm not the smartest (I will never be) or have the richest background but here's the thing, I'm too young and too ambitious not to work hard.
