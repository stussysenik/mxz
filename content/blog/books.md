Ever since, I have been an avid reader. When I was younger, I read for fun and spent countless hours of my free time in libraries. However, as I was growing, the times have changed I slowly begun to loose interest in books. For one, it was thanks to the development of technology. Naturally, a bundle of sheets of paper tightened together isn't as attractive as all those devices with 4k resolutions, face recognitions or AR/VR technologies. All these things are truly captivating.

Secondly, for a long period of time I was heavily influenced by the tips of great minds like Bill Gates. In an interview for Quartz, he mentioned a rule that he personally follows when picking books to read, but neither did I know, this advice would cause more harm than good to me.

It was this quote that I stumbled upon: 

> I don't let myself start a book that I'm not gonna finish

I was probably around 16 at that time, and it wasn't until this year, I have realized this advice doesn't work quite well for me.
